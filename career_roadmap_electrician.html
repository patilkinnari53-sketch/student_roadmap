Electrician Roadmap

Complete guide to becoming a certified electrician in India

Complete Roadmap

1

Class 10 (Minimum Qualification)

Pass 10th standard with Science and Mathematics as subjects.

2

ITI Electrician Course

Enroll in Industrial Training Institute for Electrician trade.

Duration
2 years (4 semesters)
Eligibility
10th pass with Science & Maths
Course Content
Electrical Theory, Wiring, Motors, Transformers, Safety
3

Apprenticeship (1-2 Years)

Practical on-the-job training under skilled electricians.

Apprenticeship Types
Industrial, Commercial, Residential
Stipend
₹5,000-10,000 per month during training
Areas
Wiring, Installation, Maintenance, Repair
4

Certification & Licensing

Get certified to work as a professional electrician.

NCVT Certificate
National Council for Vocational Training
Electrical Supervisor License
Required for commercial work
Wireman License
For residential electrical work
5

Specializations (Optional)

Advanced training in specific areas.

Industrial Electrician
Factories, plants, heavy machinery
HVAC Electrician
Heating, ventilation, AC systems
Solar Technician
Solar panel installation and maintenance
Automation
PLC, SCADA, industrial automation
6

Career Paths & Earnings

Electricians have stable and growing career opportunities.

Junior Electrician
0-2 years (₹15,000-25,000/month)
Senior Electrician
2-5 years (₹25,000-40,000/month)
Master Electrician
5+ years (₹40,000-60,000/month)
Self-Employed
Own business (₹30,000-1 Lakh/month)
Government Jobs
Railways, PWD, Electricity Boards (₹30,000-50,000/month)

Quick Info

  • Duration: 2 years (ITI) + 1-2 years (Apprenticeship)
  • Qualification: 10th Pass with Science & Maths
  • Course Fees: ₹5,000-25,000 per year (Govt ITI), higher in private
  • Starting Salary: ₹15,000-25,000/month

Top Recruiters

  • Indian Railways
  • State Electricity Boards
  • L&T
  • Siemens
  • BHEL
