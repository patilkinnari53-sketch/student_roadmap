Dentist (BDS) Roadmap

Complete guide to becoming a dentist in India

Complete Roadmap

1

Class 11-12 (PCB)

Take Physics, Chemistry, Biology with English. Minimum 50% marks required.

Core Subjects
Physics, Chemistry, Biology, English
Minimum Marks
50% in PCB (40% reserved)
2

Crack NEET-UG

Qualify NEET with good rank for admission to dental colleges.

Eligibility
17 years, 12th PCB
Exam Pattern
180 questions, 720 marks
3

BDS (5 Years)

Bachelor of Dental Surgery – 4 years + 1 year internship

Year 1: Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry
Year 2: Pathology, Microbiology, Dental Materials
Year 3: Medicine, Surgery, Oral Pathology
Year 4: Orthodontics, Periodontics, Prosthodontics
4

Compulsory Internship (1 year)

Rotatory internship in various dental departments.

Clinical: Oral Surgery, Orthodontics, Prosthodontics
Community: Dental camps, public health
5

Registration with Dental Council

Register with DCI or state dental council to practice.

6

Post‑graduation (MDS) – Optional

3 years specialization, NEET‑MDS required.

Clinical: Orthodontics, Oral Surgery
Diagnostic: Oral Pathology, Public Health
7

Career Paths & Earnings

Choose based on qualifications.

Private practice: ₹5-15 LPA
Govt sector: ₹6-10 LPA
Corporate: ₹4-8 LPA
Academics: ₹5-9 LPA

Quick Info

  • Duration: 5 years BDS + 3 years MDS
  • Entry Exams: NEET-UG, NEET-MDS
  • Course Fees: ₹2-10L (Govt), ₹15-40L (Private)
  • Starting Salary: ₹4-8 LPA (job) / ₹5-15 LPA (clinic)

Top Dental Colleges

  • Maulana Azad Dental College, Delhi
  • Government Dental College, Mumbai
  • Manipal College of Dental Sciences
  • CMC Vellore - Dental
