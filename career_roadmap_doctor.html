Doctor (MBBS) Roadmap

Complete guide to becoming a doctor in India

Complete Roadmap

1

Class 11-12 (PCB)

Take Physics, Chemistry, Biology with English. Aim for at least 50% in PCB (40% for reserved categories).

Core Subjects
Physics, Chemistry, Biology, English
Important Exams
NEET-UG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)
2

Crack NEET-UG

Qualify NEET with high rank to get admission in top medical colleges.

Eligibility
17 years old, 12th pass with PCB, 50th percentile
Exam Pattern
180 questions, 3 hours, 720 marks, PCB + English
3

MBBS (5.5 years)

Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery - 4.5 years academic + 1 year compulsory internship

Phase I (1.5 yrs)
Pre-clinical: Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry
Phase II (1.5 yrs)
Para-clinical: Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology
Phase III (1.5 yrs)
Clinical: Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, OBG
4

Compulsory Internship (1 year)

Rotatory internship in various departments - Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, OBG, Ortho, etc.

5

Registration with Medical Council

Register with National Medical Commission (NMC) or State Medical Council to practice as a doctor

6

Post-graduation (MD/MS) - Optional

3 years specialization. Appear for NEET-PG.

Clinical
MD in Medicine, Pediatrics, Radiology, Dermatology
Surgical
MS in Surgery, Ortho, ENT, Ophthalmology, OBG
Super-specialization
DM/MCh in Cardiology, Neurology, Urology (3 years)
7

Career Paths & Earnings

Choose your career path based on your interests and qualifications.

Government Sector
₹8-15 LPA (Junior Specialist), ₹15-25 LPA (Senior)
Private Hospitals
₹10-20 LPA (Junior), ₹20-40 LPA (Senior)
Private Practice
₹15-50+ LPA (depending on location & specialty)

Quick Info

  • Duration: 5.5 years (MBBS) + 3 years (PG)
  • Entry Exams: NEET-UG, NEET-PG
  • Course Fees: ₹5-20 Lakhs (Govt), ₹30-80 Lakhs (Private)
  • Starting Salary: ₹8-15 LPA (Junior Doctor)

Key Skills

Empathy Patience Attention to Detail Communication Problem Solving Teamwork
